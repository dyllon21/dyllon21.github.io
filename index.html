  1. SAS Somerset

    2. The SAS Somerset is the only Boom Defence Vessel left in the world and though it is not currently open to the public, it has served as a museum ship since 1988.

    The SAS Somerset was built in 1941 during the Second World War and was manned by one officer and 37 men

    In 1942 her duties commenced as servicing boom defence equipment while lying in Saldanha Bay but she was laid up in Durban in 1947and re-commissioned in 1955. In 1959 the ship’s original coal-fired engine was replaced with an oil-fired engine, but in 1967 new boilers and a reconditioned engine were installed.
